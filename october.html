On Tuesday 3rd October, it was a Feiertag (national holiday), namely reunification day: on this day in 1990, the German Democratic Republic (East) joined with the Federal Republic of Germany (West). This date marks the offical completion of the reunification. I was informed of alternative suggested date for this Feiertag: the 9th November. On this date in 1989, the Berlin wall came down and it coincided with the Proclamation of Germany, in 1918, and the defeat of the Hitler's Munich Putsch , in 1923. However, this date is not celebrated because, on this day in 1938, was the Kristallnacht, a horrific pogrom against Jews.

I spent some time researching and reflecting these events and also visited the nearby Schloss, called Schwetzingen.

It's a wonderful palace, with sprawling gardens, containing numerous "smaller" houses and sculptures.
