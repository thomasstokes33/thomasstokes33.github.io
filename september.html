Jolly days in Germany (September)

First stop (or start)... Ryanair flight

It was delayed but, in an amazing display of teamwork and ingenuity, they turned the plane around in seemingly 30 minutes. Once in Köln, I met a legend on the bus, called Sophie, with whom I chatted for the whole journey.

Day 1 (Day 0 was travel day, I hope this is ok)

The next day I worked in the morning and then headed for some local attractions:

  • Jesuitkirche
  • Church of the Holy Spirit
  • Uni Library
  • Gelato Go
  • Schloss
  • Schitzelhaus - mit zitrone
  • Alte Brücke

I then ventured back along Philosophie Weg before meeting three lovely groups:

  1. Three gentlemen who just beckoned me over for a chat. They had moved to Heidelberg from Afghanistan and they told me about their lives and taught me a little German.
  2. An older man and a lady who teaches locally, with whom I chatted with at the Neckarorte Strandbar Römerbad, where live Latin music was playing.
  3. A group of local Bachata dancers/volleyball players, who I sang with, whilst guzzling a delightful rädler.

Then I headed back home and had a fun day working remotely and going shopping. I went to the Deutsch-Amerikanisches institut. On Tuesday, I moved to Walldorf and settled into a regime of work, gym, German practice, shopping for food and exploration (plus sleep). I also met the lovely Global Sponsorship team :)

My first weekend

I chatted with James on the Friday night and we finished Avatar: The Last Airbender. On Saturday, I did some work; chatted with Millie and Will; then, I played tennis with Karl-Heinz. On Sunday, I cycled around the town and played Tennis with Jula – the host's daughter, who lives in Heidelberg – and Chris, her boyfriend.

The Chaos begins (week 1 proper)

After meeting Axel and Lisa briefly, lots of the US team and Sinead from Ireland arrived and we headed to Lars' for a party!
